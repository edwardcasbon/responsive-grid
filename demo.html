Responsive grid demo.

Build grids by using classes to describe how wide a column should span.

Customise column and gutter/margin settings in the scss before compiling to get your grid how you want it.

Note: This demo page is using a compiled version of the scss, using upto a 12 column grid with 1% gutters.

6 column grid

Markup

<div class="grid">
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-6">1 of 6</div>
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-6">1 of 6</div>
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-6">1 of 6</div>
<div class="grid-col span-2-of-6">2 of 6</div>
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-6">1 of 6</div>
</div>

Result

1 of 6
1 of 6
1 of 6
2 of 6
1 of 6

10 column, gutterless, grid with breakpoints

When using breakpoints it's best to use a gutterless grid as when the columns break over to a new line the left margin (applied to all columns apart from the 1st) prevents the column from lining in.

Pre-fix the class name with a breakpoint name (set in the .scss) to set how to display the column at certain breakpoints.

Markup

<div class="grid no-gutter">
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-10 small-span-1-of-2">1 of 10 by default, 1 of 2 on small screens</div>
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-10 small-span-1-of-2">1 of 10 by default, 1 of 2 on small screens</div>
<div class="grid-col span-1-of-10 small-span-2-of-6">1 of 10 by default, 2 of 6 on small screens</div>
<div class="grid-col span-3-of-10 small-span-1-of-1">3 of 10 by default, 1 of 1 on small screens</div>
<div class="grid-col span-4-of-10 small-span-1-of-1">4 of 10 by default, 1 of 1 on small screens</div>
</div>

Result

1 of 10 by default,
1 of 2 on small screens
1 of 10 by default,
1 of 2 on small screens
1 of 10 by default,
2 of 6 on small screens
3 of 10 by default,
1 of 1 on small screens
4 of 10 by default,
1 of 1 on small screens
